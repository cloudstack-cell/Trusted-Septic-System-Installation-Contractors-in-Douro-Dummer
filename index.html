What a Septic System Does and Why It’s Important

A septic system handles wastewater when municipal sewer access isn't available. It typically includes a septic tank to hold solids and a drain field to filter wastewater into the soil. For rural or semi-rural homeowners, this is your first line of defense in maintaining hygiene, safety, and environmental protection.

Improper systems can lead to:

  • Sewage backups
  • Foul odors
  • Water contamination
  • Health risks
  • Fines from local authorities

Experienced contractors help you avoid these issues by designing and installing systems that meet Ontario Building Code standards and suit your specific property layout and soil type.

Step-by-Step Septic System Installation Process

Here’s what to expect when working with professional contractors:

  1. Site Evaluation: A licensed expert performs a percolation test and assesses the lot’s suitability for a septic system based on drainage, setbacks, and proximity to water sources.
  2. Custom System Design: The team designs a solution based on your property and household size—factoring in tank capacity, drain field placement, and local regulations.
  3. Permit Application: They handle all the paperwork with your local township or health unit, ensuring every document is complete and compliant.
  4. Excavation & Installation: With high-grade equipment and trained operators, the team digs and installs your system with precision and care.
  5. Inspection & Final Approval: Before backfilling, a local inspector checks the system to ensure it meets all regulatory requirements.
  6. Post-Installation Guidance: Homeowners are educated on best practices—like pump schedules and signs of system issues—to keep everything running smoothly.

Common Septic Mistakes to Avoid

Even high-quality systems can fail if not used properly. Here are some pitfalls:

  • Skipping soil tests
  • Going with the cheapest bid
  • Delaying maintenance
  • Building over system components
  • Flushing grease, chemicals, or wipes

With the help of seasoned septic system installation contractors, these costly mistakes are easily avoided.

  • Uneven grass growth over the drain field
